// current research

Dissertation — IV Characterisation of Memristive Chalcogenides

Sb₂Se₃ Memristive Device Characterisation

MSc Dissertation · in progress

University of Southampton

Investigating current-voltage behaviour of antimony selenide (Sb₂Se₃) thin-film devices, framed against the von Neumann memory wall problem, with off-stoichiometry variation used to probe the link between point defects, trap states, and resistive switching hysteresis.

  • Designed and executed IV characterisation experiments across stoichiometric variants of Sb₂Se₃, distinguishing off-stoichiometry effects from elemental substitution.
  • Building a literature-grounded methodology connecting defect chemistry to switching behaviour, spanning non-volatile memory, neuromorphic/synaptic, and optoelectronic application profiles.
  • Evaluating candidate chalcogenide systems (Sb₂S₃, Sb₂Te₃, GeSe, GST, Bi₂Se₃, and the Sb₂(S,Se)₃ alloy system) for relevance to the memristive switching mechanism under study.
semiconductor devices neuromorphic computing thin-film characterisation
